UGEM Project Governance
🌌 Overview
The Universal Goal Execution Model (UGEM) is an ambitious project aiming to redefine the foundations of software execution. To maintain the integrity of our Deterministic Substrate while fostering a vibrant ecosystem, we follow a Visionary-Led Governance model.

Our goal is to balance the agility of a startup with the stability and transparency of a world-class open-source project.

🎭 Roles
Project Lead (Benevolent Dictator for Life - BDFL)
The Project Lead is the ultimate arbiter of the project.

Responsibilities: Architectural vision, final approval on GDL (Goal Definition Language) specifications, and management of the Core Substrate.

Maintainers
Maintainers are contributors who have demonstrated a deep understanding of the UGEM philosophy and a consistent track record of high-quality contributions.

Privileges: Write access to specific modules (e.g., planning/ or distributed/).

Path to Maintainership: Maintainers are invited by the Project Lead after demonstrating leadership within the community and codebase.

Contributors
Anyone who submits code, documentation, or feedback.

Privileges: Recognition in CONTRIBUTORS.md and a voice in the direction of the project via UIPs.

🏛 Decision-Making Process
We prioritize Consensus-Seeking but maintain Strong Leadership to prevent "design by committee" which can degrade architectural purity.

1. UGEM Improvement Proposals (UIPs)
For significant changes (new GDL features, core kernel refactors, or new AGPL modules), we use the UIP process:

Propose: Start a thread in GitHub Discussions under the "Proposals" category.

Discuss: The community provides feedback, stress-tests the logic, and identifies edge cases.

Decide: The Project Lead or a designated Module Maintainer makes the final call to accept, defer, or reject the proposal.

2. Small Changes & Bug Fixes
Small fixes and documentation updates follow the standard Pull Request (PR) process and do not require a formal UIP.

⚖️ Licensing Enforcement
Governance ensures the Split-License Model (AGPL v3 / MIT) is strictly followed.

Any PR that moves logic from the Substrate (AGPL) to the Surface (MIT) will be rejected unless it is clearly an interface definition.

This ensures UGEM remains a protected public good.

🤝 Evolution of Governance
As UGEM reaches Production Readiness, the governance model may evolve into a Steering Committee or a Foundation model to support enterprise-scale adoption. For the Developer Preview phase, the Visionary-Led model ensures the project stays true to its core principles of Determinism and Intent.
